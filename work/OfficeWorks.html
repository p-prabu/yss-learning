The Key Disciplines

Mental rules to keep the system strict and simple while you process.

  • Thinking (Clarification): Do not just scan; understand. DIFFERENTIATE between simple information and required tasks. Ask yourself: "What is this?" and "What do I need to do?"
  • Organizing (Sorting): Once you have thought about the email, immediately categorize it into one of the designated folders (Action, Reading, Waiting). Do not leave it in the Inbox.
  • Execution Strategies:

    The 2-Minute Rule: If an email takes less than two minutes to answer or handle, do it immediately. Do not sort it.

    The Sort Rule: If it takes more than two minutes, move it to the Action folder to do later.

The 5 Essential Email Categories

Sort every email into one of these to keep the inbox flowing:

  • Action: Emails that require you to perform a specific task, respond, or complete an assignment. Rule: Anything that takes more than 2 minutes goes here.
  • Reading: Information you want to consume for knowledge or interest but that requires no immediate action or reply.
  • Waiting: Messages where you delegated work or are awaiting a response/information before you can proceed.
  • Archive: Keep-for-reference emails or compliance items; no action needed now, but store them.
  • Trash: Not needed, spam, or subscriptions to cancelтАФdelete immediately.
